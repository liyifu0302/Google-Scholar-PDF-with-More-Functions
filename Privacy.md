# Privacy Policy for PaperPilot

Effective date: [insert date]

PaperPilot is a Chrome extension designed to improve academic PDF reading. Its single purpose is to provide an enhanced PDF reader for academic papers, including table, figure, and appendix previews, author links, two-page view, keyword location markers, highlighting, drawing, and annotation tools.

## Data We Process

PaperPilot may process the following data to provide its reader features:

- PDF URLs and related page information, used to detect and open PDF documents in the enhanced reader.
- PDF content displayed in the reader, used to support text selection, table/figure/appendix preview, search, highlighting, and annotation.
- User-created reader data, such as highlights, notes, drawings, and reader preferences.
- Reader interaction data, such as feature usage or reader events, used only to provide, maintain, and improve the PDF reading experience.

## How Data Is Stored

Reader preferences, highlights, annotations, drawings, and related PDF reading data may be stored locally in the user's browser using Chrome storage, localStorage, or IndexedDB.

## Data Sharing and Transfer

PaperPilot does not sell user data.

PaperPilot does not use user data for advertising, creditworthiness, lending, or unrelated purposes.

If Google Scholar integration or highlight synchronization is used, limited reader data may be sent to Google Scholar only for the purpose of supporting PDF reading, highlight synchronization, or related reader functionality.

## Remote Code

PaperPilot does not use remote code. All JavaScript, CSS, PDF libraries, and reader features are packaged inside the extension.

## Permissions

PaperPilot requests browser permissions only to provide its PDF reader features, including detecting PDF documents, opening PDFs in the enhanced reader, saving reader preferences, supporting annotations, copying selected content, and exporting or downloading user-created outputs.
